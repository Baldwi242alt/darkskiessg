Having lived in Singapore my entire life, I grew accustomed to a night sky dominated by artificial light. From my home in Tampines, true darkness never exists — the skyline remains illuminated in an endless amber glow, obscuring much of the natural night sky from view.
During a school excursion to Cameron Highlands in 2024, I experienced something I had never seen before. Stepping outside late at night, I looked up to find a sky filled with countless stars, with the Milky Way visible across the horizon in remarkable clarity. It was a powerful moment that completely changed my perspective on what urban environments like Singapore have gradually lost through excessive nighttime lighting.
Returning home afterwards made the contrast impossible to ignore. Looking up from my HDB corridor, the stars were once again hidden behind the city’s constant glow. That experience transformed light pollution from an abstract environmental issue into something deeply personal. It reinforced my belief that sustainable urban lighting is not only about energy efficiency, but also about restoring humanity’s connection with the natural world, protecting biodiversity, and preserving the night sky for future generations.
